Unit 01 · 1–2

Hello & Who I Am

Greet and respond, introduce myself, give personal information, spell my name.

WIDA SI Social & Instructional Key Use Inform

Introduce myself and give personal information

Target structures

  • My name is ___.
  • I am from ___.
  • I speak ___.
  • This is ___.

Foundational literacy strand

The alphabet as names of letters, not yet sounds. Recognize and write my own name accurately. Letter formation for the letters in my name and country.

Vocabulary — core

hello hi goodbye name my your from speak yes no please thank you nice to meet you

Vocabulary — academic

introduce information country language first name last name

Teaching note

Name order varies by culture — in many countries the family name comes first. Ask how a student wants to be called and say it correctly; a mispronounced name on day one is the fastest way to lose a newcomer. Some students have a name they use at home and one assigned at a border or camp. Let them choose.

Unit 02 · 3–4

My School

Places in the building, school staff and their jobs, supplies, reading my schedule.

WIDA SI Social & Instructional Key Use Inform

Locate places and people in my school and say where I need to go

Target structures

  • Where is the ___?
  • The ___ is ___ (next to / near / upstairs).
  • I need to go to the ___.
  • This is Ms./Mr. ___. She/He is the ___.

Foundational literacy strand

Letter–sound correspondence for initial consonants using school words. Reading numerals on doors and matching them to a written schedule.

Vocabulary — core

office nurse bathroom cafeteria library gym classroom hallway teacher principal counselor pencil paper notebook backpack

Vocabulary — academic

schedule period room number locate staff attendance

Teaching note

School structures differ enormously. Passing periods, lockers, bells, and choosing your own lunch may all be new. Some students come from schools where you stay in one room all day, and some have never been in a school building at all. Walk the building physically before teaching the words.

Unit 03 · 5–6

Classroom Language

Follow common commands, ask for help, understand yes/no and wh- questions.

WIDA SI Social & Instructional · LA Language Arts Key Use Inform

Follow classroom directions and ask for what I need to participate

Target structures

  • Imperatives: Open ___. Take out ___. Turn to ___.
  • Can you repeat that, please?
  • I don't understand.
  • How do you say ___ in English?
  • Yes/no questions vs. wh- questions (what, where, who, when)

Foundational literacy strand

Sight words for high-frequency classroom verbs. Recognizing question words in print by their initial wh- pattern.

Vocabulary — core

open close take out put away listen look write read stand up sit down line up page book help repeat slowly again

Vocabulary — academic

direction instruction question answer example practice turn in due

Teaching note

In many cultures saying 'I don't understand' to a teacher is disrespectful, or admitting confusion causes loss of face — so students nod and smile while understanding nothing. Teach and explicitly celebrate help-seeking as correct school behavior. This unit is the single highest-leverage one in Phase A: it unlocks every mainstream class the student sits in.

Unit 04 · 7–8

Numbers, Time & Calendar

Count, days and months, tell time, talk about my daily schedule.

WIDA SI Social & Instructional · MA Mathematics Key Use Inform

Say and understand times, dates, and quantities in school life

Target structures

  • It is ___ o'clock. / It is ___ thirty.
  • What time is ___?
  • My ___ class is at ___.
  • Ordinal numbers: first, second, third…
  • on Monday / in January / at 3:00

Foundational literacy strand

Number words in print alongside numerals. Writing the date in US format and recognizing that it differs from the day/month order used in most of the world.

Vocabulary — core

numbers 1–100 o'clock half past morning afternoon night today tomorrow yesterday days of the week months early late before after

Vocabulary — academic

schedule calendar date duration quantity digit equals total

Teaching note

Date format is a real source of error and embarrassment: 03/04 is March 4th here and April 3rd almost everywhere else. Teach it explicitly. Twelve-hour time with a.m./p.m. is also unfamiliar to students from 24-hour-clock countries. Some SLIFE students need genuine number-sense work, not just the English words — coordinate with the math teacher rather than assuming the concept is present.

Unit 05 · 9–10

Feelings & Health

Name emotions and body parts, say "I need…," visit the nurse, basic health.

WIDA SI Social & Instructional Key Use Inform

Say how I feel and what I need

Target structures

  • I am ___ (tired / sick / okay).
  • I have a ___ache.
  • My ___ hurts.
  • I need ___.
  • Do you need ___?

Foundational literacy strand

Body-part labeling with a diagram. The -ache compound pattern (headache, stomachache, toothache) as a spelling and meaning family.

Vocabulary — core

happy sad tired sick hungry thirsty cold hot scared okay head stomach throat hand arm leg hurt pain nurse medicine water rest

Vocabulary — academic

symptom injury emergency describe health wellness appointment

Teaching note

Handle this unit with care. Newcomers may have experienced trauma, injury, or loss in transit, and questions about the body and how you feel can surface it. Never require a student to disclose. Keep the emotional vocabulary factual and low-stakes, offer a non-verbal way to opt out, and know your school's referral path before you teach it. Also: describing pain to an adult is culturally variable — some students are taught to endure it silently. Say plainly that telling the nurse is the right thing to do here.

Unit 06 · 11–12

Family & Home

Family members, describe people, rooms and where I live.

WIDA SI Social & Instructional Key Use Inform, Narrate

Describe the people I live with and where I live

Target structures

  • I live with ___.
  • This is my ___.
  • He/She is ___ (tall / kind / twelve).
  • I have ___ brothers and ___ sisters.
  • There is / There are ___.

Foundational literacy strand

Possessive 's in print. Compound words for rooms (bed-room, bath-room) as a decoding pattern.

Vocabulary — core

mother father sister brother grandmother grandfather aunt uncle cousin baby house apartment room kitchen bedroom bathroom live together

Vocabulary — academic

family relative household describe appearance generation guardian

Teaching note

This unit can be painful. Many newcomers are separated from parents, live with distant relatives or a sponsor, are unaccompanied minors, or have lost family members. Never assign a family tree as required work, never ask who a student lives with in front of peers, and always offer an alternative: describe a person who is important to you. Also avoid assuming a nuclear family — extended and multi-household families are the norm for most of the world.

Worked task · Speaking — “Explain a goal and how you'll reach it”

Level 1 · Entering

“I want goal.”

ScaffoldChooses a picture from a goal bank and says the frame with one slot filled. The goal can be anything — finish this class, play soccer, get a job.

Level 2 · Emerging

“I want to goal because reason. I need to step.”

ScaffoldTwo-sentence frame linking a goal to a reason and one action. The because is now expected, not optional.

Level 3 · Developing

“My goal is to goal. To do that, I need to step and step. The hard part is obstacle, so I'm going to strategy. Could you help me with specific request?”

ScaffoldOpen frame chaining goal → steps → obstacle → strategy → request. This is the whole sequence's destination: a student who can advocate for themselves with an adult in an institution, in English, unprompted.
